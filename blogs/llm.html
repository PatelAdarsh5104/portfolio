Understanding Large Language Models (LLMs)

Published on April 25, 2024

Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized the field of Artificial Intelligence. These models, like GPT-4, Llama 3, and Claude, are trained on massive datasets to understand and generate human-like text.

How do they work?

At their core, LLMs are based on the Transformer architecture. They use attention mechanisms to weigh the importance of different words in a sentence, allowing them to capture long-range dependencies and context.

Applications

From code generation to creative writing and language translation, LLMs are being integrated into almost every software domain.

Future of LLMs

The future lies in making these models more efficient, less biased, and capable of handling multimodal inputs like images and videos.
